Austin Area CCL 2017/2018 Holiday Newsletter

Happy new year, and welcome to the first edition of the Austin area CCL members’ newsletter, a new activity we're kicking off this year. Each month we will send a newsletter to local CCL members with the latest achievements and goals of CCL’s Austin area chapters.

I am your newsletter compiler, Shalmali Joshi. I'm a volunteer in the West/Central chapter. If you have questions or suggestions, please email me!

Grasstops News

Austin City Mayor Steve Adler and District 9 Council Member Kathie Tovo are our newest endorsers in November. Austin CCL Grasstops is planning a push for a City Council resolution endorsing the CCL's Carbon Fee and Dividend policy.

In January, our grasstops coordinators Mike DeChellis and Mike Thompson are creating an endorser training program and a grasstops support group for CCL Austin. If you are interested in joining, contact them at michael.craig.thompson@gmail.com or michael@dechellis.org.

Social Media Accomplishments

Mike Zangrilli has taken over the Austin area Facebook Page and is doing terrific work building our following. The CCL Austin Facebook page currently has 200 followers. We will continue to add followers by encouraging volunteers to engage their friends and continue the outreach process through other Austin Eco-friendly groups.

Visit the overhauled Austin CCL Facebook page to join, follow and share with your community!

Lobbying Efforts

Multiple local volunteers travelled to Washington D.C. for Congressional Education Day in November. They had in-person meetings with Rep. John Carter's Legislative Director, Rep. Roger Williams’ legislative staff, and Rep. Lamar Smith’s office.

Media Outreach

In November, local members successfully published two letters to the editor with the Austin American-Statesman and four with the Williamson County Sun. The letters explored questions related to revenue neutral carbon pricing and distribution of net proceeds back to households.

Grassroots Outreach

We've had an impressive run of tabling events lately. On November 7, CCL presented at the Austin Downtown Rotary. Two presentations and a tabling event were held at Southwestern University, which produced 74 constituent comments forms. A tabling event was also organized at Juli Berwald's book launch of 'Spineless: The Science of Jellyfish and the Art of Growing a Backbone' as well as at Planet Texas 2050. The Barton Springs chapter travelled to Johnson City and Kerrville for tabling events at the Science Mill and Kerr Market Days.

Citizens' Climate Lobby is co-hosting a free Climate Change Forum with CleanTX and Austin EcoNetwork on Jan 23, 2018. Among the speakers is Zach Baumer, Climate Program Manager with the Office of Sustainability, City of Austin, TX and local CCL volunteer Mike Thompson.

Chapter News

Round Rock/Georgetown: We added three new members. The chapter also presented to the Round Rock Sunrise Rotary on December 1, 2017 and the Sun City Rotary Club on Jan 9, 2018.

Barton Springs: We added several new members, and we continue working to arrange presentations throughout our congressional district (21).

West/Central: We hope to continue on our success with Letters to the Editor from earlier in the year, and we’ll be participating with the Barton Springs chapter presenting at the Circle C Democrats meeting on January, 8, 2018.
